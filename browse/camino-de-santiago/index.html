Camino de Santiago

The Camino de Santiago, also known as the way of Saint James, is the name of any of the pilgrimage routes to the town of Santiago de Compostela in northern Spain. El Camino was one of the most important Christian pilgrimages during medieval times and even today many take up this route as a form of spiritual path or retreat.
